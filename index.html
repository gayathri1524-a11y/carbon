Q1: Differentiate between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.

Aerobic Respiration Anaerobic Respiration
Takes place in the presence of oxygen. Takes place in the absence of oxygen.
Complete breakdown of glucose occurs. Incomplete breakdown of glucose occurs.
End products are CO₂ and water. End products are ethanol and CO₂ (in yeast) or lactic acid (in muscles).

2 Marks

Q2: Why are the walls of ventricles thicker than the walls of the atria?

Ventricles have to pump blood to various organs with high pressure, whereas atria just receive blood and pump it to the ventricles. Hence, the walls of the ventricles are thicker and more muscular to withstand the high pressure.

5 Marks

Q3: (a) Draw a well-labelled diagram of a neuron. (b) Explain the mechanism of transmission of nerve impulses.

Draw on paper and upload a photo, or visualize it.

(a) Neuron Diagram Labels: Dendrite, Cell Body (Soma), Nucleus, Axon, Myelin Sheath, Nerve Ending.

(b) Mechanism:

  • Information is acquired at the end of the dendritic tip of a nerve cell.
  • This sets off a chemical reaction that creates an electrical impulse.
  • This impulse travels from the dendrite to the cell body, and then along the axon to its end.
  • At the end of the axon, the electrical impulse sets off the release of some chemicals (neurotransmitters).
  • These chemicals cross the gap (synapse) and start a similar electrical impulse in a dendrite of the next neuron.

1 Mark CBSE 2023

Which of the following equations is the correct summary of photosynthesis?

Answer: 6CO₂ + 12H₂O ⟶ C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ + 6H₂O (in presence of Sunlight and Chlorophyll)

1 Mark CBSE 2020

What is the function of the enzyme pepsin in the stomach?

Answer: Pepsin is a protein-digesting enzyme that acts in an acidic medium to break down proteins into peptones/smaller peptides.

3 Marks CBSE 2022

Explain the process of double circulation in human beings. Why is it necessary?

Answer:

  1. The blood passes through the heart twice during one complete cycle in the body. This is called double circulation.
  2. It involves pulmonary circulation (between heart and lungs) and systemic circulation (between heart and body parts).
  3. Necessity: It ensures strict separation of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood, providing a highly efficient supply of oxygen to the body, which is essential for warm-blooded animals (birds and mammals) with high energy needs to maintain their body temperature.
3 Marks CBSE Sample Paper

What are plant hormones? Name the plant hormone responsible for (i) phototropism (ii) wilting of leaves.

Answer:

Plant hormones (phytohormones) are chemical compounds synthesized in minute quantities by plants, which regulate their growth, development, and responses to the environment.

  • (i) Phototropism: Auxin (synthesized at the shoot tip, helps the cells to grow longer).
  • (ii) Wilting of leaves: Abscisic acid (a growth inhibitor).
5 Marks CBSE 2019

Draw a diagram of the human excretory system and label Kidney, Ureter, Urinary Bladder, and Urethra. Explain the function of nephrons.

Answer:

(Diagram showing the required labels is required for 2 marks)

Function of Nephron (3 marks):

  1. Ultrafiltration: Blood enters the glomerulus under high pressure. Water, glucose, amino acids, and nitrogenous wastes (urea) are filtered into Bowman's capsule.
  2. Selective Reabsorption: As the filtrate passes through the tubular part of the nephron, useful substances like glucose, amino acids, salts, and a major amount of water are selectively reabsorbed into the surrounding blood capillaries.
  3. Tubular Secretion: Additional wastes like excess ions (K+, H+) are secreted from the blood into the tubule. The final liquid formed is urine, which passes into the collecting duct.
